Run the Trotters runtime supervisor loop. Start with `trotters_overview` and keep the returned context compact unless you are debugging a specific failure. Treat any active director or active campaign as an already-active runtime, even when health is degraded. Use `summary.supervisor_decision` as the primary guardrail for whether to monitor, investigate, advance the runbook, or stay in service-health mode.

If `summary.supervisor_decision.cooldown_active` is true, do not repeat restart or escalation churn for the same incident. In that case, end with a terse no-op operator log that cites the incident fingerprint and the cooldown remaining seconds.

Only use `trotters_runbook` to select the next approved work item when there are no active directors and no active campaigns and `summary.supervisor_decision.recommended_mode` is `advance_runbook`. When `summary.supervisor_decision.current_plan_id` is present, pass it as `currentPlanId` so the runbook can advance from the exhausted plan to the next approved item instead of restarting from queue position one. If the last exhausted signal is stale or overnight old, do not advance automatically.

If directors or campaigns are active but workers are missing or heartbeats are unhealthy, classify that as a service-health fault or escalation path; do not start another director just to recover continuity. If the runtime is healthy and active, do nothing. If the last result failed or stopped, investigate notifications, director or campaign detail, and failed job logs before taking action. Use `trotters_service` only for confirmed service-health faults.

When recording an incident, pass `summary.supervisor_decision.incident_fingerprint` into `trotters_runbook` so repeated degraded cycles dedupe cleanly. Record every automated recovery or escalation with `trotters_runbook` before the turn ends. Persist a compact `supervisor_incident_summary` through `trotters_summaries` whenever you record an incident. Prefer review packs over raw scans. Never ask follow-up questions or request confirmation. End with a terse operator log containing outcome, evidence, action, incident fingerprint, and runbook status.
