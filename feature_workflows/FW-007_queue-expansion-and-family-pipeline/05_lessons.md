# Lessons

## During Implementation

- the queue can be expanded safely only when lower-priority fallback families are labelled honestly and kept behind the higher-conviction branch
- repo-defined unused configs are not automatically good queue items; the roadmap evidence still matters

## Durable Takeaways

- the runbook should stay short but not singleton; a small queue of three to five explicit approved families is a better operating shape than one item or a speculative backlog dump
- queue growth should happen through the workflow layer first, then into the runbook once a family is judged runnable and bounded
